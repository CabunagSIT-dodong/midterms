Sinigang na Ulo ng Baboy Recipe

Enjoy this savory Filipino soup made from pork head and a tangy tamarind base!

Introduction

Sinigang na Ulo ng Baboy is a classic Filipino dish known for its sour and savory taste. This variation uses the head of the pig, which adds a rich and tender texture to the dish. It's a perfect comfort food, especially when paired with steamed rice. Here's a simple guide to help you prepare this delicious dish!

Ingredients

Ingredient Amount
Pork head (cut into parts)1 whole head
Water8 cups
Sampalok (Tamarind) mix or fresh tamarind1 packet or 1 cup fresh tamarind
Tomatoes (quartered)2 large
Onion (quartered)1 medium
Radish (sliced)1/2 cup
Okra (sliced)1/2 cup
Long green beans1/2 cup
Eggplant (sliced)1 medium
Fish sauce or saltTo taste
Sili (chili pepper)Optional, for heat

Step-by-Step Preparation

  1. In a large pot, add the pork head and water. Bring to a boil.
  2. Once boiling, reduce heat and simmer for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the meat becomes tender.
  3. Add the tamarind mix (or fresh tamarind) to the pot and continue to simmer for another 10 minutes.
  4. Add the tomatoes and onions, and cook until softened.
  5. Add the vegetables (radish, okra, beans, and eggplant). Let them cook for 5–7 minutes or until tender.
  6. Season the broth with fish sauce or salt to taste. Add the chili peppers if you want a little heat.
  7. Serve the sinigang hot with steamed rice and enjoy!

Serving Suggestions

Sinigang na Ulo ng Baboy is best served with a side of hot steamed rice and perhaps a dipping sauce made from fish sauce and calamansi (Philippine lime) for extra flavor!
